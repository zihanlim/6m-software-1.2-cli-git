ABOUT

Lim Zi Han (08 April 1993) is a Singaporean Architectural Designer.

EARLY LIFE AND EDUCATION

Zi Han was born in Singapore, in 1993 to second-generation chinese parents of the Fujian Province.

In 2014, he began his study in Architecture and design at the National University of Singapore, where he graduated with a Masters of Architecture and was awarded the NUS-JTC i3 Centre Innovation Medal and Prize for Architecture for Best design in 2018. His thesis work in Architecture was later recognized again in 2019 and was selected as one of the Thesis design work to represent the university at thedesign showcase in National Cheng Kung University (NCKU), Taiwan.

After graduation, he progressed into architecture design, starting his formal career. Constant musings of form, space, beauty and function

CAREER

Zi Han worked as an Architectural designer for a number of international Architectural design consultancy.

During this time, Zi Han worked internationally across asia in Beijing, Hong Kong and Singapore.

In 2019, Zi Han was employed by Ma YanSong, and worked for him at his architecture design studio in Beijing as an intern and then later as an Architectural Assistant. Subsequently, ZiHan worked for other design boutiques such as UNStudio and Foster+Partners.

Musings

After studying and working in Architecture and Urban Planning for several years, Lim Zi Han recognized the significant potential for automation within the AEC industry. Motivated by this realization, he aspires to transition from AEC to programming and pursue the fields of Machine Learning and Computer Vision.

The journey was challenging, as Lim Zi Han dedicated all his available time to learning programming. He completed online courses, undertook projects and collaborations, read research papers, and attended AI-related courses at the National University of Singapore. Despite the difficulties, the experience was both amazing and enjoyable.

Currently, Lim Zi Han works in the design industry, predominantly in building architecture, fulfiling various design and industry processes using 3D modelling and 2D drafting techniques in the fields of architecture and urban design.

While he possesses confidence in his work, Lim Zi Han remains open to questioning and reevaluating established practices. He acknowledges that there is still much to learn and continues to dedicate his free time to keeping up with the latest advancements in the field. Learning new concepts and staying up-to-date is not only fulfilling but also a source of enjoyment for him.
